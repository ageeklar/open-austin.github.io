In 2012, Code for America founder Jen Pahlka was invited to to SXSW Interactive give a keynote address. Every year since, SXSW has presented many sessions of interest to the civic technology community.

Currently, SXSW is soliciting public input on session proposals through their Panel Picker tool. You can vote a proposal up (or down) to show your support (or not), as well as leave comments. Registration is required, but it's free. Even if you haven't registered to attend the event, you can participate in the panel picker.

We'd like to highlight some of the sessions of interest that we know about.

Aug 30 update: Innovation Office proposals added.

If you are aware of any additional session proposals that may be of interest to the local civic tech community, please email us at info@open-austin.org or tweet us at @OpenAustin. We'll post updates as warranted.

Open Austin

Each year for the past two years, Open Austin has hosted a meetup for open government hackers and civic technology enthusiasts. We'd like to do it again this year. To support our proposal, please register for the SXSW Panel Picker and vote it up.

Civic Tech Hackers & Advocates Meetup
http://panelpicker.sxsw.com/vote/55660

Austin CityUP

Austin CityUP is a new smart city consortium being formed. We are one of the founding members. There are a couple of interesting session proposals from CityUP participants.

http://panelpicker.sxsw.com/vote/56682

http://panelpicker.sxsw.com/vote/56757
